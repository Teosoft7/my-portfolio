# Predicting the winner

Let's address a long-standing debate among baseball fans through data:
**"Is a championship won with hitting or pitching?"**

Using 11 seasons of data from 2015 to 2025 (since the 10-team system was established), comprising 71 team-level seasonal metrics (batting, pitching, fielding, and baserunning) provided by the official KBO record center, I analyzed the conditions required to win the KBO regular season.

---

## What Did the Champions' Records Look Like?

Over these 11 seasons, the 1st-place team in the regular season averaged approximately **88 wins** with a **.613 winning percentage**—a 12.6 percentage point advantage over the rest of the league (average .487).

However, the spectrum of winning percentages among regular-season champions is surprisingly broad:

- **2016 Doosan Bears:** Dominated the league with a **.650** winning percentage.
- **2021 KT Wiz:** Clinched 1st place with a **.563** winning percentage in a fiercely contested season.

### Key Metrics Comparison: Champions vs. Rest of the League

| Metric                  | Champion Average | Other Teams Average |
| :---------------------- | :--------------: | :-----------------: |
| **Runs Scored / Game**  |     **5.78**     |        4.96         |
| **Runs Allowed / Game** |     **4.62**     |        5.09         |
| **OPS**                 |    **0.799**     |        0.751        |
| **ERA**                 |     **4.22**     |        4.68         |

> **Takeaway:** Unsurprisingly, teams that consistently score more runs and allow fewer runs win championships.

---

## Hitting vs. Pitching: Which Matters More?

Comparing the explanatory power (coefficient of determination, R²) of ERA and OPS—the representative single metrics for pitching and hitting—on winning percentage:

- **ERA >> Win Rate:** R² = 0.35 (Weak individual explanatory power)
- **OPS >> Win Rate:** R² = 0.26 (Weak individual explanatory power)
- **OPS + ERA >> Win Rate:** R² = 0.83 (Explanatory power increases dramatically!)

While the old adage says "baseball is a pitcher's game," the data shows that achieving a regular-season championship requires essential **complementarity between hitting and pitching**.

---

## What Run Differential Doesn't Tell Us: "Clutch Luck"

Using **Pythagorean Expectation**—a model used to estimate expected win percentages from runs scored and allowed—we see that actual team outcomes vary significantly by season:

- **2018 Doosan Bears (+6.6 wins) & 2024 KIA Tigers (+7.0 wins):** Won noticeably more games than expected by excelling in close, high-leverage games.
- **2021 KT Wiz (-9.5 wins):** Finished 1st place with 9.5 fewer wins than expected, reflecting a season of intense, tightly matched competition across the league.

---

## Predicting Mid-Season Performance

Ultimately, baseball is about outscoring the opponent. Although Pythagorean expectation explains much of what makes a strong team, sabermetrics still leaves room for predictive modeling.

To build on this, I developed a machine learning model to predict the regular-season champion and postseason contenders using comprehensive team metrics.

### Model Design & Feature Selection

To enable **mid-season predictions**, counting metrics that scale with games played (e.g., hits, HRs, runs allowed) were excluded. Instead, the model focuses exclusively on **rate metrics**:

- **Selected Features:** OPS, RISP (BA with runners in scoring position), ERA, WHIP, etc.
- **Standardization:** Applied **Z-score normalization** per season to evaluate relative performance within the league, minimizing the impact of changing league environments (e.g., ball elasticity changes).

### Model Performance (Leave-One-Season-Out CV)

Evaluated across 11 iterations using Leave-One-Season-Out cross-validation, the Logistic Regression model achieved:

| Performance Metric                          | Result                          |
| :------------------------------------------ | :------------------------------ |
| **Regular Season Champion Top-1 Accuracy**  | **7 out of 11 Seasons (63.6%)** |
| **Postseason Top-5 Qualification Accuracy** | **Avg 4.18 / 5 Teams**          |
| **AUC (Championship)**                      | **0.94**                        |

## Regular Season 1st Place vs. Korean Series Champion

An important nuance to note: **The "Champion" label in this dataset represents 1st place in the regular season, not the Korean Series winner**.

Across the 11 seasons, there were two postseason upsets where the regular-season winner did not win the Korean Series:

1. **2015:** Regular Season 1st: _Samsung_ $\rightarrow$ Korean Series Champion: _Doosan_
2. **2018:** Regular Season 1st: _Doosan_ $\rightarrow$ Korean Series Champion: _SK_

The **2018 season** offers a fascinating insight into baseball strategy:

- Doosan outperformed SK in overall Win Rate (.646 vs .542) and OPS (.862 vs .829).
- However, **SK possessed superior pitching with a better ERA (4.67) than Doosan (4.98)**.

> **Data Insight:** Over a 144-game marathon, overall roster depth and offense win out. But in a short 7-game postseason series, elite pitching dominance often creates upsets.

## Limitations & Live Dashboard Deployment

### Limitations

- **Sample Size:** A dataset of 110 team seasons (11 champion labels) is relatively small for deep statistical inference.
- **Unquantifiable Variables:** Qualitative factors like official ball elasticity changes (e.g., 2019 de-juiced ball), mid-season injuries, and trades are hard to capture purely through rate metrics.

### Live Dashboard

To put this model into practice, I deployed an automated weekly dashboard that calculates mid-season championship and postseason qualification odds using rate metrics.
